I came across the work of Arjan Janssen on a site Professor Hermon was raving about; ‘butdoesitfloat.com is a resource you should all know of, it is filled with great images’ he said with tremendous surety & jurisdiction. In retrospect, he was right. I recall almost immediately having my attention arrested by the bare, disparate paintings that met my gaze once the webpage had finally loaded. Usually, I don‘t bat my eyelashes twice at fine art pieces that fall in the class of “things the vast majority of people would say they can make themselves...” but this time, things were different. I was captivated by the broad, powerful lines of black paint that Janssen used to mar the canvas, the unremitting “empty” spaces that took up most of the works entirely. What was most startling yet alluring is that beyond the aesthetic components of Janssen‘s art, which left me in awe, I felt such wealth of emotion. I connected with the pieces even though I couldn‘t necessarily deduce anything from them   —
